Long Island Ice Tea

Description

The Long Island Ice Tea is a classic cocktail known for its potent mix of spirits with a deceptively innocent appearance. Despite its name, this drink contains no actual tea - it gets its amber color from a splash of cola. This infamous cocktail was allegedly created in the 1970s in Long Island, New York, and has since become a staple at bars worldwide.

The combination of multiple spirits makes this one of the strongest cocktails you can order, typically containing around 22% alcohol by volume. Its smooth, slightly sweet taste masks its strength, making it both popular and notorious. When properly made, it should taste refreshing and balanced, with no single spirit overpowering the others.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 oz vodka
  • 1/2 oz white rum
  • 1/2 oz silver tequila
  • 1/2 oz gin
  • 1/2 oz triple sec
  • 1 oz fresh lemon juice
  • 1 oz simple syrup
  • Cola to top
  • Lemon wedge for garnish
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
  2. Add the vodka, rum, tequila, gin, triple sec, lemon juice, and simple syrup to the shaker.
  3. Shake well for about 15 seconds until thoroughly chilled.
  4. Fill a highball glass with fresh ice.
  5. Strain the mixture from the shaker into the glass.
  6. Top off with cola, pouring slowly to create a layered effect.
  7. Gently stir to combine the ingredients.
  8. Garnish with a lemon wedge.
  9. Serve immediately and enjoy responsibly.
